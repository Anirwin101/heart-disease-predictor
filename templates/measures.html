Heart Insight

Understanding your health numbers is an important step toward protecting your heart. This page explains the common clinical and physiological parameters used in Heart Insight, along with typical healthy reference ranges. These values help you interpret what each measurement generally represents in cardiovascular health.

The information provided here is intended for education and awareness purposes only. Individual health conditions vary, and these ranges should not be treated as a diagnosis. If any of your values fall outside typical ranges or you experience concerning symptoms, consulting a qualified medical professional is strongly recommended.

Chest Pain Type (0–3): Chest pain is a symptom, not something to "reduce." However, preventing underlying causes like blocked arteries can help avoid dangerous chest pain. This means managing cholesterol, blood pressure, weight, stress, and avoiding smoking.

Value Meaning Typical Risk Insight
0 Typical Angina Higher concern
1 Atypical Angina Moderate concern
2 Non-anginal Pain Lower concern
3 Asymptomatic Can still be risky

Resting Blood Pressure (mm Hg): High blood pressure strains the heart and damages arteries. Risk can be reduced by limiting excess salt, exercising regularly, maintaining a healthy weight, sleeping well, managing stress, and avoiding smoking or excessive junk food.

Healthy target: ~ 110–120 mm Hg

Category Systolic (mm Hg)
Normal < 120
Elevated 120–129
High (Stage 1) 130–139
High (Stage 2) ≥ 140

Serum Cholesterol (mg/dl): Elevated cholesterol contributes to artery blockage. Lowering risk involves reducing trans fats and excessive saturated fats, increasing fiber intake, staying physically active, and choosing healthier fats (nuts, seeds, olive oil).

Healthy target: 150–200 mg/dl

Category Total Cholesterol (mg/dl)
Desirable < 200
Borderline High 200–239
High ≥ 240

Fasting Blood Sugar > 120 mg/dl: High blood sugar can damage blood vessels over time. Keeping levels healthy involves reducing excessive sugar intake, staying active, maintaining a healthy weight, and following a balanced diet with controlled carbohydrates.

Healthy target: 70 - 99 mg/dl

Category Fasting Glucose (mg/dl)
Normal < 100
Prediabetes 100–125
Diabetes Range ≥ 126

Resting ECG Results (0–2): ECG findings reflect heart electrical activity and cannot be directly "reduced." The focus should be on preventing heart damage through blood pressure control, cholesterol management, exercise, and early medical evaluation if abnormalities appear.

Healthy: 0 (Normal ECG)

Value Meaning
0 Normal ECG
1 ST-T wave abnormality
2 Possible ventricular hypertrophy

Maximum Heart Rate Achieved: A low exercise capacity may indicate poor cardiovascular fitness. Improving heart health involves regular aerobic exercise like walking, cycling, sports, or swimming — gradually increasing stamina strengthens the heart.

General formula: Estimated Max HR = 220 – Age

Example: Age 15 → ~205, bpm Age 40 → ~180 bpm

✅ Healthy sign: Higher achievable HR during exercise (good fitness)

⚠️ Very low values may suggest reduced exercise capacity

Exercise Induced Angina: This is a warning sign of reduced blood flow to the heart. Prevention strategies include improving fitness, managing cholesterol, controlling blood pressure, reducing stress, and avoiding smoking.

Healthy: No exercise induced angina

ST Depression (Oldpeak): ST depression suggests stress-related heart strain. While the value itself isn't "reduced" by lifestyle alone, improving cardiovascular health through exercise, weight control, and risk-factor management can improve heart function over time.

Healthy: 0 (Normal)

Value Interpretation
0 Ideal / Normal
0.1 – 1.0 Mild
> 1.0 Increasing concern

Slope of Peak Exercise ST Segment (0–2): This reflects ECG response to exercise and is not directly modifiable. Long-term heart health improvements via fitness, diet, and medical management may positively influence such parameters.

Value Meaning
0 Downsloping
1 Flat
2 Upsloping

Number of Major Vessels (0–4): This represents arterial blockage — not something users can adjust themselves. Preventive care is crucial: healthy diet, exercise, cholesterol control, and avoiding smoking reduce progression of vessel disease.

Value Meaning
0 No visible blockage
1–4 Increasing vessel involvement

Type of Thalassemia (1–3): This is genetic and cannot be changed. Individuals with inherited conditions benefit from regular medical monitoring and maintaining optimal cardiovascular health through lifestyle management.

Value Meaning
1 Normal
2 Fixed defect
3 Reversible defect
